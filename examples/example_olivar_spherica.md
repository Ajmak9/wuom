# Example — Olivar (Spherica)

This example shows how WUOM can be applied using the Spherica geometry in an olive grove.

## Situation

An olive grove is not only a system of elements and processes.

It is also a field of relationships:
- between trees 
- between species 
- between soil and atmosphere 
- between observation and perception 

## Geometry

Spherica

## Structure

Instead of linear or grid-based organization, 
the system is understood as a sphere of interactions.

### Core

- Olive tree as central organism 

### Inner Layer

- Roots 
- Soil life 
- Water retention 
- Microorganisms 

### Middle Layer

- Tree structure 
- Leaves 
- Fruit 
- Immediate biodiversity 

### Outer Layer

- Climate 
- Light 
- Wind 
- Seasonal cycles 
- Human observation 

## Result

All elements are interconnected without hierarchy.

The system is perceived as a whole rather than as separated parts.

This allows:
- holistic observation 
- better timing of actions 
- reduced intervention 

## Notes

Spherica is not about structure. 
It is about relationship.

WUOM here becomes perception.
